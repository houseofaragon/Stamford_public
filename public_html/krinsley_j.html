James S. Krinsley, MD

James S. Krinsley MD, FCCM, FCCP is Director of Critical Care at Stamford Hospital and Clinical Professor of Medicine at Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons. He graduated Summa Cum Laude from Yale College with Distinction in American Studies. He subsequently graduated from Cornell University Medical College before completing Internal Medicine training at New York University and Pulmonary and Critical Care Medicine training at Yale University School of Medicine. He joined Pulmonary Associates of Stamford in 1986. He has had an active office practice, caring for a diverse group of patients with respiratory conditions such as asthma, COPD, interstitial lung disease and unexplained cough or shortness of breath. He has also been Director of Critical Care at Stamford Hospital since 1998. In 2002 the Institute for Healthcare Improvement and National Coalition on Health Care named the Stamford Hospital Intensive Care Unit as one of 11 best practices in the United States. He is very involved in the teaching of medical and surgical house staff and has won numerous teaching awards. In addition, he has conducted research in various aspects of critical care medicine, with a primary interest in the control of blood glucose in the critically ill. The interventional trial of intensive insulin therapy completed at Stamford Hospital resulted in Stamford Hospital’s receipt of the Codman Award for hospital quality by the Joint Commission in 2004. Dr. Krinsley’s publications have explored the clinical impact of hyperglycemia, hypoglycemia, glucose variability and diabetes in the acutely and critically ill, and he has been an invited lecturer on these topics at numerous national and international conferences.
