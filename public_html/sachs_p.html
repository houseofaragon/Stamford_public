Paul Sachs, MD

Dr. Paul Sachs is a true New Yorker. Dr. Sachs grew up in Queens and graduated Summa Cum Laude / Phi Beta Kappa from Queens College. He then went to New York University School of Medicine where he graduated with Alpha Omega Alpha honors. He then completed a three year Residency in Internal Medicine at New York Hospital, Weill Cornell Medical Center where he served as an Assistant Chief Resident. He completed his fellowship in Pulmonary and Critical Care Medicine at Albert Einstein / Montefiore Medical Center In 1987.

Dr. Sachs then moved to Connecticut and joined Pulmonary Associates of Stamford. He is now the Director of the Pulmonary Division, Pulmonary Rehabilitation, and the Pulmonary Function Laboratory. He is a clinical preceptor for Columbia University medical students and is very involved in medical education of The Stamford Hospital house staff. In 1996 he established the outpatient Pulmonary Rehabilitation Program which is now located at The Tully Center. He is a Principal Investigator of many clinical trials looking for innovative therapeutic options for patients with chronic lung diseases. In 2014 Dr. Sachs was recognized by his physician peers at Stamford Hospital as being a “Role-Model Physician.”

College: Queens College, City University of New York

Interests: COPD, emphysema, asthma, shortness of breath, lung nodules, smoking cessation, community education, pulmonary rehabilitation and clinical research

Appointments: Director Pulmonary Medicine, Stamford Hospital Associate Clinical Professor of Medicine, Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons

All physicians at Pulmonary Associates are board certified in critical care medicine and provide the highest patient centered care for an array of pulmonary conditions
