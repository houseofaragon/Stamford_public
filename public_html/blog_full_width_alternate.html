Michael A. Bernstein, MD

Dr. Michael Bernstein graduated Magna Cum Laude and Phi Beta Kappa from Duke University. He remained at Duke and graduated from Duke University School of Medicine in 2003. Dr. Bernstein then moved to New York City where he completed a combined residency in internal medicine and pediatrics where he served as the chief resident. He then remained at Mount Sinai to complete a fellowship in pulmonary, critical care, and sleep medicine. In 2010, Dr. Bernstein joined Pulmonary Associates of Stamford. Having been raised in Fairfield, Dr. Bernstein was delighted to return to Fairfield County. Dr. Bernstein leads the bronchoscopy/advanced diagnostic pulmonary procedures team at Stamford Hospital and serves as the Associate Director of Critical Care Medicine. He is very involved in housestaff as well as medical student education. Dr. Bernstein’s clinical interests include critical care, lung cancer, pulmonary nodules, COPD, adolescent asthma, and sleep apnea.

College: Duke University

Medical School: Duke University School of Medicine

Residency: Internal Medicine and Pediatrics, Mount Sinai Medical Center

Fellowship: Pulmonary, Critical Care, and Sleep Medicine, Mount Sinai Medical Center

Board Certification: Internal Medicine, Pediatrics, Pulmonary Disease, Critical Care Medicine, Hospice and Palliative Care

Interests: Critical Care, Lung Cancer, Pulmonary Nodules, Bronchoscopy, COPD, Adolescent Asthma, Sleep Apnea

Appointments: Associate Director Critical Care Medicine, Stamford Hospital; Assistant Clinical Professor of Medicine in Stamford—Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons

All physicians at Pulmonary Associates are board certified in critical care medicine and provide the highest patient centered care for an array of pulmonary conditions
